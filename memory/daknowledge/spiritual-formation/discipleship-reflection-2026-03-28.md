# Discipleship Formation Reflection
**Date:** March 28, 2026  
**Context:** Spanish-language church training / Mass formation  
**Topic:** Discipleship, Prayer, and Orthodoxy

---

## Set 1: Prayer, Orthodoxy, and Leadership

### Question 1
**Spanish:** ¿Has tenido que tomar decisiones importantes en las que recurres a Dios en la oración? ¿Cuál fue tu experiencia?

**English:** Have you had to make important decisions where you turn to God in prayer? What was your experience?

**Robert's Response:**
> "Yes, most major decisions are made while asking God to provide Guidance. Right now, I am looking for a house and I dont know how I will achieve it but I ask the Lord to Guide and have me walk the right path he wants for me. My experiences are always emotional and beautiful."

**Reflection:** Trust in providence during uncertainty; seeking God's will in practical life decisions (housing). Acknowledges the emotional and spiritual beauty of surrendering to divine guidance.

---

### Question 2
**Spanish:** Ahora que conoces la importancia de la ortodoxia (fieles a la doctrina y autoridad de la Iglesia), ¿te consideras un discípulo que dedica tiempo, espacio y dinero a la formación y la oración?

**English:** Now that you know the importance of orthodoxy (faithful to the doctrine and authority of the Church), do you consider yourself a disciple who dedicates time, space, and money to formation and prayer?

**Robert's Response:**
> "No one could ever dedicate enough time, space, and money; but we strive for it, for in giving do we receive, only in how we act will there be salvation. I try and I cry."

**Reflection:** Deep humility—recognizing human limitations in the face of infinite calling. Echoes the spiritual principle of sacrificial giving ("in giving do we receive"). The phrase "I try and I cry" captures the struggle and sincerity of the spiritual journey.

---

### Question 3
**Spanish:** ¿Cómo puedes ayudar a otros líderes y/o ministerios cuando notas que caminan lejos de la doctrina de la Iglesia?

**English:** How can you help other leaders and/or ministries when you notice they are walking far from the Church's doctrine?

**Robert's Response:**
> "Escuchanlos -> Listen to them and know you are equally as guilty, if not today or yesterday, but maybe tomorrow. Tell them you love them."

**Reflection:** Radical humility in correction—approaching others with awareness of one's own fallen nature. Prioritizes relationship and love over judgment. "Tell them you love them" as the foundation for any correction.

## Set 2: Communion with Pastors and Path to Holiness

### Question 4
**Spanish:** ¿Cómo describo mi comunión y obediencia con mi párroco y mi Obispo como mis pastores más inmediatos?

**English:** How do I describe my communion and obedience with my parish priest and my Bishop as my most immediate shepherds?

**Robert's Response:**
> "I never volunteered or signed up to help, but I shan't hesitate when asked. I always help when I can and it feels right."

**Reflection:** Humble availability—open to serving without seeking recognition. Responsive obedience rather than self-appointed leadership. Trusts inner discernment ("when it feels right") guided by the Spirit.

---

### Question 5
**Spanish:** ¿Cuales son las formas en que ayudas a nutrir y guiar en tu ministerio/grupo?

**English:** What are the ways in which you help nurture and guide in your ministry/group?

**Robert's Response:**
> "By always showing up and being prepared when it is my time/turn to be an Usher."

**Reflection:** Faithfulness in the "small" things—presence and punctuality as spiritual discipline. The ministry of showing up. Being an Usher as a sacred role of welcome and order.

---

### Question 6
**Spanish:** Lee la cita bíblica de Hebreos 12:14 y comparte sobre el camino a la santidad en tu vida y de tu ministerio.

**English:** Read the biblical quote from Hebrews 12:14 and share about the path to holiness in your life and your ministry.

**Scripture:**
> "Make every effort to live in peace with everyone and to be holy; without holiness no one will see the Lord." — Hebrews 12:14 (NIV)

**Robert's Response:**
> "It began with a woman, how now transitioned to a wife. But my faith now lies with our Lord. I have found a calming in where I can hear and listen to people. Lord grace me with your patience and love for all things."

**Reflection:** Journey from worldly attachment to spiritual devotion. Marriage as part of the sanctification journey. The gift of listening—calming presence for others. Prayer for patience and universal love (agape).

---

## Themes Emerged

1. **Trust in Providence** — Surrendering outcomes to God (house search)
2. **Imperfect Striving** — "I try and I cry" — the authentic struggle of faith
3. **Humility in Correction** — Knowing we share the same fallen nature
4. **Love as Foundation** — Leading with love before doctrine
5. **Sacrificial Giving** — Time, space, money as acts of worship
6. **Responsive Availability** — Serving when asked, not self-appointed
7. **Faithfulness in Small Things** — Showing up, being prepared as Usher
8. **Journey to Holiness** — From attachment to devotion; listening as ministry
